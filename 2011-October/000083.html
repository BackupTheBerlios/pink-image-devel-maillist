On 9 Oct 2011, at 9 Oct, 22:40, Laszlo Marak wrote:

> Dear Pink Users!
> 
> Berlios has announced that it would cease it's services on 2011-12-31. Berlios 
> hosts our mailing list and the bug-tracker (which is of less interest as 
> nobody's using it at the moment). It would also host the mercurial repository 
> which we had wanted to switch to. We are once again in a seek of a project 
> management system. 
> 
> Here are the proposed constraints (there are discussable):
> 
> We would like to have a public mercurial repository. Anyone should be able to 
> pull the repository. 
> 
> We would like to have a mailing list where anybody can send messages without 
> inscription. (The non subscribed users may require authorization).
> 
> We would like to have a bug-tracker, where anyone can post a bug (even if it's 
> idle at the moment it can become useful some time). It would be good if users 
> could send bug reports without a subscription.
> 
> In any case the subscription can ONLY enforce an email address as requirement 
> (no phone number, no bank card id, no gmail account, no yahoo account,  etc.)
> 
> We would prefer free services but paid subscriptions are also to consider 
> (free as in free speech not free beer). 
> 
> If You have suggestions or experiences please share them with the rest of the 
> mailing list owners.
> 
> sincerely Yours,
