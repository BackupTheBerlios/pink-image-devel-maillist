Currently it is difficult to compile the command-line version of pink, it seems it relies on boost-python even though it shouldn't require it. The compilation simply fails while building the library.

A workaround it to request the compilation of *both* the command-line and python version of pink. In this way the compilation goes through normally. The resulting version of pink-python is unusable (it segfaults) but the command-line version works.
