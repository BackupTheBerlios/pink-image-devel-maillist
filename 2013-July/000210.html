Dear Kacper, 

we are very delighted to see You being interested in Pink.  About the versions 
that You have been maintaining: mercurial (the source code manager) of Pink is 
fully capable of managing Your separate versions of Pink. It is also capable 
of pulling the new changes from us (the default, main branch) and keeping Your 
changes. For this You do not need any access. You can also save Your changes 
into the repository on Your machine and still receive the changes from pinkhq.

About Your patches: I will review Your changes, but the simplest long-term 
solution is to give You access to the Pink repository. There is a testing 
repository, which is less developed as the library itself, and I can give You 
access to that repository too. 

To learn to use mercurial, I would just ask You to prepare Your patches in 
"mercurial" form  using the following tutorial:

http://mercurial.selenic.com/wiki/Workflows#One-off_patch_submission

after I have reviewed Your changes I can give You full access to the mercurial 
repository and You can merge back Your patches if You like.

I hope to hear about You soon, 

Cordially, 

Laszlo
